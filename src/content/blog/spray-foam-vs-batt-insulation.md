In a steel barndominium, insulation isn't an afterthought — it's the difference between a building that fights the Texas heat and one that shrugs it off. Metal conducts temperature fast, so how you seal and insulate the envelope sets the tone for comfort and energy bills for years.

## The case for spray foam

Closed-cell spray foam does two jobs at once: it insulates and it air-seals. In a metal building, that combination is hard to beat. It fills gaps, locks out moisture, and keeps conditioned air where it belongs.

## Where batt still makes sense

Batt insulation is cheaper up front and can work well in the right assembly. The trade-off is air-sealing — batt alone won't stop the drafts and condensation that steel buildings are prone to.

## Our take

For most of the barndos we build, a tight, foam-sealed envelope pays for itself in comfort and lower bills. We'll always walk you through the options for your specific build and budget.
